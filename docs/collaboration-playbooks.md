# Collaboration Playbooks

This document outlines practical collaboration models between Digital Legacy Weaver and partner applications.

## Core collaboration principle

Digital Legacy Weaver orchestrates release policy and operational safety.  
Partner applications remain source systems for accounts, asset metadata, and domain-specific workflows.

## Playbook 1: Crypto + Exchange + Wallet

1. User registers connector for exchange/wallet provider.
2. User stores encrypted asset references (not plaintext secrets).
3. Partner receives release event acknowledgement workflow.
4. Beneficiary follows controlled unlock flow with verification step.
5. Beneficiary/legal entitlement verification is completed directly with destination provider process.

Value:

1. clear recovery map for heirs
2. reduced risk of secret leakage
3. auditable release timeline

## Playbook 2: Cloud and productivity accounts

1. User links cloud/storage connectors.
2. User defines which folders or account references matter for legacy.
3. Self-recovery mode protects owner lockout scenarios.
4. Legacy mode provides guided handover references.

Value:

1. preserves continuity for family or executor
2. avoids chaos from scattered account ownership

## Playbook 3: Legal office collaboration

1. Law office keeps legal will process and executor validation.
2. Digital Legacy Weaver stores digital operation references and release pathways.
3. During execution, office uses logs and references to perform verified digital steps.

Value:

1. legal process remains legal
2. digital operations become actionable and trackable

## Playbook 4: Family office / trustee operations

1. Trustee registers connectors for managed platforms.
2. Policy and reminder controls reduce accidental release risk.
3. Incident controls allow immediate pause and structured recovery.

Value:

1. governance-ready digital succession
2. stronger control and audit discipline

## Collaboration guardrails

1. Do not position product as legal will replacement.
2. Keep legal disclaimers and consent in onboarding/release settings.
3. Keep secrets encrypted and minimize transmission scope.
4. Use incident and safety controls during anomalies.
5. Use standard handoff wording from `docs/provider-handoff-template.md`.
