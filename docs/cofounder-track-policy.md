# Co-Founder Track Policy (v1)

This policy defines how high-performing collaborators can become co-founders in the future.

## Intent

1. Reward sustained high ownership and strategic impact.
2. Keep promotion criteria transparent and merit-based.
3. Protect project continuity and governance quality.

## Eligibility window

Candidate must satisfy all baseline conditions:

1. active contribution period >= 6 months
2. top-tier contribution performance for >= 3 consecutive distribution periods
3. no unresolved conduct or trust violations

## Evaluation dimensions

Candidate must demonstrate ownership in at least 2 of these domains:

1. product direction and roadmap execution
2. reliability/security and incident leadership
3. ecosystem/partner integration impact
4. release governance and operational discipline

## Quantitative threshold (recommended)

For the last 3 completed periods:

1. candidate performance points >= 20% of total team points in each period
2. at least 1 high-impact contribution per period (incident/security/critical release)

## Qualitative threshold

1. trusted in crisis response
2. consistent collaboration behavior
3. can lead cross-functional decisions without supervision

## Nomination and approval flow

1. nomination submitted in writing
2. evidence packet attached (points, contributions, incident ownership)
3. owner review and decision
4. if approved, co-founder role effective date is documented

## Co-founder package (default template)

When promoted, co-founder receives:

1. role/title recognition in project governance docs
2. elevated decision scope in agreed domains
3. compensation adjustment by owner-approved addendum

## Governance notes

1. promotion is not automatic by points alone
2. owner retains final appointment authority in v1
3. policy can evolve with formal governance upgrades
